Taylor & Francis

Analytical Study on Consumer Social and Behavioral Psychology and Its Influence on Online Purchasing

After an year of thorough research, we focused on the booming world of online shopping. With more people buying goods online, it was crucial to understand why they make the choices they do. We dug into data from online purchases and website visits to see what it could tell us about consumer behavior and decision-making. This chapter zooms in on key moments in online shopping and how people's personalities affect what they buy. We also looked at how data analysis is changing the game in understanding consumer behavior online. Our goal is to make sense of how people shop online today and how their personalities shape their decisions, using insights from our research.

IEEE (SMARTGEN CON) - 2021

Optimal Face Recognition System using Haar Classifier

This paper addresses a solution to implement voting system using facial recognition using this model which has a commendable accuracy of 96%. In our country, voting takes place with the help of EVM's but there is no prior surviellance to justify if the voter who is voting the same as in the electoral list. Our proposed approach acts as a bridge and helps to cater this issue using OpenCV specifically Haar classifier. This research particularly focuses on the use of Haar cascades and the LBPH Face recognizer algorithm as a hybrid combination for the smart voting system as an authentication approach.

IEEE (GCAT) - 2021

Leveraging Market Sentiment for Stock Price Prediction using GAN

Stock market is a heavily explored subject in the realms of Machine Learning and Deep Learning. With thousands of people investing into the stock market and many more joining the suit lured by its lucrativeness or by the idea of paying lower taxes, it has become necessary to read this stochastic time series data, popularly known as the stock price data. Through this paper, we want to display most of the recent research done in this domain and propose a new state-of-the-art GAN architecture that consists of a hybrid of Naive Bayes Classifier for Sentiment Analysis of Financial News and LSTM for prediction as the Generator and MLP as the Discriminator. The proposed system forecasts stock values using financial data and conducts sentiment analysis on financial news.

Springer (IACC) - 2021

Machine Learning-Based Psychology: A Study to Understand Cognitive Decision-Making

With the rise in the adaptability of emotions and technology in humans; we as humans have seen a lot of transformation in our individual thought process. With the help of machine learning algorithms, we aim at understanding how a certain user responds to questions asked him from different categories which can help us understand his/her behavior.Hence, the ideology behind this research is crystal clear in determining the psychological state of the audience from varied age groups categorized as youths, adults, and millennials.

Springer (RCAAI) - 2022

Machine Learning for Prediction of Nutritional Psychology, Fast Food Consumption and Its Impact on Students

The intake of fast food is rapidly accelerating due to the factors specifically the cost-effectiveness and being tasty, but not all individuals are aware of its harmful long-term effects on physical and mental health. On the issue of nutrition and fast food intake this is an unexplored field of research so far. In this paper, we have profoundly analyzed the relationship using Machine Learning models, which is a new approach for nutrition-based analysis. The primary objective is to apply association, classification, and regression algorithms in order to predict BMI, sickness, pre-COVID and post-COVID eating schedule and the experiments conducted during this research reveal that this method significantly improves the analysis of real-world data as compared to the traditional statistical approach with a commendable accuracy of 98%.
